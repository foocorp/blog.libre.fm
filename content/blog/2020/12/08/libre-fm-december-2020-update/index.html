Libre.fm December 2020&nbsp;update

8 December, 2020

Usually I write these updates in January on the years where I don’t forget, this year I thought I’d write something in December to talk a little about where we are and what we’re doing next.

But first, the numbers:

  • 244,932,201 listens.
  • 141,715,189 unique tracks.
  • 310,300 users.
  • 118,823 users active.

Streaming services continue to dominate the way people listen to music and many have no facility for scrobbling. I recommend Web Scrobbler if this is your use case (it’s also available for Chromium based browsers) and you can easily scrobble to multiple services with it, and from a myriad of different sources of music.

Now onto the good stuff.

Libre.fm is getting a software refresh!

For the first time in over a decade, Libre.fm is getting a brand new software stack — the whole site is going to be rewritten in Laravel, a modern web framework that still lets us maintain compatibility with PHP. Using a modern framework will let us add some useful missing features to the site, including the all-important export features we desperately need, but also should see us into the future for another decade.

GitHub?

Will the development happen on GitHub? Who knows. Probably not, to be honest. But for now, please leave us your ideas, feature requests, things you hate, things you love on GitHub. If you hate GitHub, please leave them on GitLab.com. I suspect more people have GitHub accounts however, so its less of a barrier to entry.
